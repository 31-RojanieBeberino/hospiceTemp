Hospice care is a highly specialized form of care designed to help anyone with a life-limiting illness make the most of their final months. It combines pain and symptom management with emotional and spiritual support to help patients live the remainder of their lives in comfort and dignity, surrounded by those they love.

About Us

Walla Walla Community Hospice is an independent, not-for-profit organization established in 1980. We provide end-of-life comfort care, family support, and grief and bereavement support in the communities we serve.

Our Mission

The mission of Walla Walla Community Hospice is to provide compassionate care to patients in the final stages of life and support for their loved ones and caregivers..

Our Team

Walla Walla Hospice offers compassionate end-of-life care, creating a warm environment for patients and loved ones to find solace and support during this challenging journey.

Grief Support

Our experienced team offers compassionate companionship through grief, honoring your spirit in silence or through shared stories. We're here to walk this journey with you.

Our Services

Hospice provides a comprehensive team approach to caring for patients and families during the entire final stage of an illness, not just the very end. From the moment someone learns of an incurable condition, hospice can begin offering a full range of support services to meet all of their needs throughout this difficult time.

  • Coordination of care with patient's primary physician
  • Regularly scheduled RN visits
  • On-call RN nights and weekends
  • Hospice Aide assistance
  • Social work support
  • Spiritual support
  • Volunteer support
  • Bereavement support
  • Respite care
  • Durable medical equipment
  • Provision of personal care supplies
  • Provision of medications relating to the terminal prognosis

Patients of any age and with any terminal illness are eligible.

Clinical Care Services

Hospice provides comprehensive clinical care services, including coordinated care with the patient's doctor, regular nursing visits, on-call nursing support, and hospice aide help. They also ensure patients receive necessary medications related to their terminal condition.Learn more

Supportive Services

Hospice offers a range of emotional and spiritual support services, including social work, spiritual counseling, volunteer assistance, and bereavement care to help patients and families cope during this difficult time. Learn more.

Supplemental Care And Resources

In addition to clinical and support services, hospice also provides practical assistance such as respite care, the loan of medical equipment, and supplies for the patient's personal care needs. Learn more.

Volunteer

As a volunteer for Walla Walla Community Hospice, whether you are working directly with patients, in the office, or during an event, YOU ARE AN ESSENTIAL PART OF THE TEAM which allows us to offer quality, compassionate care to our patients and support for their caregivers or loved ones.

If you would like to volunteer, please fill out our general volunteer application form by clicking on the button below or contact Luke at luke@wwhospice.org(?) to determine what opportunities best suit your interests, abilities and availability.

Is volunteering right for me?

One of the most valuable skills our volunteers possess is compassion. Here are some things to consider before you apply:

  • Can I make a commitment to be there for others?
  • How much time can I reasonably give?
  • Where will my strengths be put to best use?

Who can volunteer?
Volunteers come from all walks of life – they are business professionals, retirees or students. Whether you can volunteer for two hours a day or two hours a week, we welcome you to our team.

  • Anyone over 18 years old can apply to volunteer.
  • No experience is necessary to volunteer. Volunteers receive training prior to starting any volunteer duties.
